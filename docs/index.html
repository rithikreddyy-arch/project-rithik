Pushing the Boundaries of Computational Biology

\n

Project Rithik is a research-grade framework for AI-driven molecular biology. We focus on problems that haven't been solved before, combining cutting-edge deep learning with mechanistic biochemistry.

🧪 De Novo Enzyme Design: The Flagship Project

\n \n
\n

Designing Novel Enzymes from Scratch

\n

Generate protein sequences that fold into functional enzymes and catalyze target reactions—a problem that hasn't been solved at scale.

\n
\n\n

The Challenge

\n

Current filters (pLDDT, backbone RMSD, motif RMSD) are necessary but insufficient. They answer \"did it fold like I asked?\" but not \"is the catalytic geometry actually correct?\"

\n

A design can pass all structural filters with 0.6 Å motif RMSD while the general base sits 1.4 Å from where it must be to catalyze—a fatal but invisible error.

\n\n

Our Solution

\n

Train an E(n)-equivariant GNN to score catalytic competence directly:

\n
CatalyticGeometryNet\n ├─ Input: atom-level graph of pocket\n ├─ Head 1: p(catalytic) classification\n └─ Head 2: distance regression (auxiliary task)
